L'Abbé Résina / La Bérézina 7c Bas Cuvier

Pierre Richard
slopers
  • Circuit
  •     white ED+ : 6 quat

To the right of Le Carnage (white n°6 ter).
NB: L'Abbé Résina is the first 7c of Fontainebleau (1983), opened with a double dyno (morpho). The first repeat was done with the classic toe-hook method, by Jacky Godoffe. Several other methods with a static start and the name La Bérézina became popular. The most extreme being a direct 7c/7c+ variant without the slopers on the left and without the characteristic sloper of Apothéose by Thomas Carbonne.
